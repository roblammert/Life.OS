# APPENDIX_ALIGNMENT_ENGINE.md  
**Alignment Engine — Life.OS**  
*Part of the Life.OS Architecture Documentation Suite*

The Alignment Engine evaluates how well the user’s actions, habits, routines, goals, concepts, emotional patterns, cognitive cycles, and identity themes fit together. It identifies alignment, misalignment, drift, and opportunities for realignment across the user’s life. Its purpose is to help the user live in a way that feels coherent, intentional, and true to who they are becoming.

## Purpose

The engine provides a unified understanding of *how aligned the user’s life is* across multiple dimensions—values, identity, goals, routines, habits, tasks, and context. It helps Life.OS generate insights, recommendations, and reflections that support coherence, reduce friction, and strengthen long‑term direction.

---

## Alignment Architecture

The engine uses a layered architecture:

- **Value layer** — alignment with core values  
- **Identity layer** — alignment with identity themes and roles  
- **Goal layer** — alignment with short‑term and long‑term goals  
- **Behavior layer** — alignment with habits, tasks, and routines  
- **Context layer** — alignment with emotional, cognitive, and productivity states  
- **Narrative layer** — alignment with the user’s personal story  
- **Evolution layer** — tracking alignment changes over time  

This architecture ensures alignment is holistic and dynamic.

---

## Alignment Data Model

The engine stores:

- **alignment_record_id**  
- **alignment_type** — value, identity, goal, behavioral, contextual, narrative  
- **alignment_score** — degree of alignment  
- **alignment_text** — human‑readable summary  
- **supporting_evidence** — entries, tasks, habits, insights, Life Moments  
- **misalignment_signals** — friction, drift, conflict  
- **alignment_opportunities** — areas for improvement  
- **evolution_metadata** — changes over time  

Alignment is measured continuously and contextually.

---

## Alignment Types

The engine evaluates alignment across several dimensions:

- **Value alignment** — actions and patterns that reflect core values  
- **Identity alignment** — behaviors that reinforce identity themes  
- **Goal alignment** — consistency between actions and goals  
- **Behavior alignment** — coherence between habits, routines, and intentions  
- **Context alignment** — fit between actions and emotional/cognitive state  
- **Narrative alignment** — alignment with the user’s personal story and meaning  

Each type provides a different lens on coherence.

The engine identifies misalignment patterns such as:

- Value conflict

- Conceptual fragmentation  
- Productivity bottlenecks  

Misalignment is treated as a signal for reflection, not failure.
