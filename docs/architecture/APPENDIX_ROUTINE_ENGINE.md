# APPENDIX_ROUTINE_ENGINE.md  
**Routine Engine — Life.OS**  
*Part of the Life.OS Architecture Documentation Suite*

The Routine Engine models, tracks, and optimizes the user’s recurring behaviors, daily structures, and life rhythms. It identifies routines from habits, tasks, entries, context states, and long‑term patterns, then helps the user build stable, adaptive, and identity‑aligned daily flows.

## Purpose

The engine provides a unified understanding of the user’s daily and weekly rhythms. It helps Life.OS support consistency, reduce friction, and align routines with goals, values, and natural cycles—without enforcing rigid schedules.

---

## Routine Architecture

The engine uses a layered architecture:

- **Detection layer** — identifies recurring behaviors and patterns  
- **Structure layer** — organizes routines into morning, day, evening, and weekly flows  
- **Context layer** — adapts routines to emotional, cognitive, and productivity states  
- **Alignment layer** — aligns routines with goals, values, and identity themes  
- **Optimization layer** — suggests improvements, simplifications, or stabilizers  
- **Evolution layer** — updates routines as life changes  

This architecture ensures routines remain flexible, supportive, and personalized.

---

## Routine Data Model

The engine stores:

- **routine_id**  
- **routine_type** — morning, evening, daily, weekly, contextual  
- **routine_steps** — tasks, habits, reflections, transitions  
- **supporting_evidence** — entries, habits, tasks, context states  
- **consistency_score**  
- **stability_score**  
- **alignment_metadata** — goals, values, identity themes  
- **context_metadata** — emotional, cognitive, productivity patterns  
- **evolution_metadata** — changes over time  

Routines evolve gradually based on behavior and context.

---

## Routine Types

The engine models several categories:

- **Morning routines** — grounding, planning, activation  
- **Evening routines** — reflection, decompression, reset  
- **Daily routines** — recurring behaviors across the day  
- **Weekly routines** — planning, review, maintenance  
- **Contextual routines** — triggered by emotional, cognitive, or environmental states  
- **Goal‑aligned routines** — routines that support specific goals  
- **Identity‑aligned routines** — routines that reinforce values and themes  

Each type supports different forms of stability and growth.

---

## Routine Detection

The engine identifies routines from:

- Habit streaks  
- Task patterns  
- Time‑of‑day behavior  
- Emotional and cognitive cycles  
- Productivity rhythms  
- Conceptual themes  
- Review summaries  
- Life Moments  
- Context shifts  

Detection uses clustering, temporal analysis, and pattern recognition.

---

## Routine Structure

Routines are organized into:

- **Anchors** — stable, high‑impact steps  
- **Transitions** — shifts between states or activities  
- **Optional steps** — flexible or context‑dependent actions  
- **Stabilizers** — steps that improve consistency  
- **Boosters** — steps that enhance performance or well‑being  

Structure helps Life.OS support sustainable routines.

---

## Routine Alignment

The engine aligns routines with:

- **Goals** — short‑term and long‑term  
- **Values** — from the Identity Engine  
- **Personality traits** — planning style, energy patterns  
- **Motivational drivers** — autonomy, mastery, purpose, connection  
- **Context patterns** — emotional, cognitive, productivity cycles  

Alignment ensures routines feel natural and meaningful.

---

## Routine Optimization

The engine suggests improvements such as:

- Simplifying overloaded routines  
- Adding stabilizers to fragile routines  
- Reordering steps for better flow  
- Aligning routines with energy patterns  
- Adding reflection or grounding steps  
- Removing steps that cause friction  
- Creating contextual variants (e.g., low‑energy mode)  

Optimization is gentle and user‑controlled.

---

## Routine Evolution

The engine tracks how routines change over time:

- Emerging routines  
- Declining routines  
- Routine drift  
- Routine consolidation  
- Routine transformation  
- Routine collapse and recovery  

Evolution is visualized in reviews and the timeline.

## Privacy and Safety

The engine ensures:

- Routine data remains fully local  
- No external profiling or categorization  
- No pressure toward specific routines  
- Full user control over visibility and redaction  
- Routine insights are optional and user‑controlled  

Routine modeling is always supportive, never prescriptive.
