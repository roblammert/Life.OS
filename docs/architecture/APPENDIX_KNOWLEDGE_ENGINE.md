# APPENDIX_KNOWLEDGE_ENGINE.md  
**Knowledge Engine — Life.OS**  
*Part of the Life.OS Architecture Documentation Suite*

The Knowledge Engine extracts concepts, themes, relationships, and semantic meaning from all user-generated content. It transforms journal entries, notes, tasks, habits, insights, and contextual metadata into a structured conceptual understanding of the user’s inner and outer world.

## Purpose

The Knowledge Engine gives Life.OS the ability to understand *what the user is talking about*—ideas, topics, themes, people, places, goals, values, and identity elements. It powers conceptual reasoning, theme detection, semantic search, and long‑term identity modeling.

## Knowledge Architecture

The engine uses a layered architecture:

- **Concept extraction layer** — identifies concepts and entities  
- **Theme layer** — groups related concepts into themes  
- **Relationship layer** — identifies semantic and contextual links  
- **Evolution layer** — tracks how concepts and themes change over time  
- **Integration layer** — connects concepts to the Graph, Memory, and Review Engines  

This architecture enables deep semantic understanding.

## Knowledge Data Model

The engine stores:

- **concept_id**  
- **concept_label** — normalized concept name  
- **concept_type** — idea, value, person, place, project, topic  
- **concept_embedding** — vector representation  
- **theme_id** — optional cluster assignment  
- **related_concepts** — semantic neighbors  
- **supporting_evidence** — entries, insights, Life Moments  
- **evolution_metadata** — how the concept changes over time  

Concepts form the building blocks of the Life Graph.

## Concept Extraction

The engine identifies:

- Ideas and topics  
- Values and beliefs  
- Goals and aspirations  
- People and relationships  
- Places and environments  
- Projects and responsibilities  
- Emotional or cognitive themes  
- Behavioral patterns  
- Identity-related concepts  

Extraction uses embeddings, semantic similarity, and contextual cues.

## Theme Detection

The engine groups concepts into themes:

- Personal growth  
- Work and productivity  
- Relationships  
- Health and well‑being  
- Creativity  
- Spirituality  
- Identity development  
- Emotional resilience  

Themes evolve as new concepts appear or shift.

## Relationship Mapping

The engine identifies relationships such as:

- **Semantic similarity** — concepts with similar meaning  
- **Contextual co‑occurrence** — concepts appearing together  
- **Causal links** — one concept influencing another  
- **Emotional associations** — concepts tied to emotional states  
- **Cognitive associations** — concepts tied to clarity or load  
- **Behavioral associations** — concepts tied to habits or tasks  

These relationships feed directly into the Graph Engine.

## Concept Evolution

The engine tracks:

- Frequency changes  
- Emotional tone shifts  
- Cognitive clarity changes  
- Contextual associations  
- Theme migrations  
- Concept drift or stabilization  

Concept evolution is used in reviews and long‑term memory.

## Knowledge Insights

The engine generates insights such as:

- concept_emerging  
- concept_declining  
- theme_emerging  
- theme_shifting  
- conceptual_breakthrough  
- conceptual_conflict_detected  
- identity_theme_detected  

These insights support the Life Coach and Review Engine.

## Knowledge Life Moments

Life Moments occur when:

- A major new concept emerges  
- A theme shifts significantly  
- A conceptual breakthrough appears  
- A long‑term conceptual conflict resolves  
- A new identity theme forms  

These moments anchor conceptual evolution in the timeline.

## Knowledge in Reviews

The engine contributes to:

- Daily reviews — emerging concepts  
- Weekly reviews — theme shifts  
- Monthly reviews — conceptual arcs  
- Yearly reviews — identity themes and life chapters  

Concepts and themes shape the narrative structure.

## Knowledge Graph Integration

The engine creates:

### Nodes
- concept  
- theme  
- conceptual_pattern  
- conceptual_arc  

### Edges
- relates_to  
- co_occurs_with  
- influences  
- associated_with  
- evolves_from  

This enables semantic navigation and conceptual reasoning.

## Knowledge and the Memory Engine

The Knowledge Engine provides:

- Conceptual evidence for long‑term memories  
- Theme evolution data  
- Identity-related conceptual patterns  
- Conceptual arcs for life chapters  

Memory and knowledge reinforce each other.

## Knowledge and the Embedding Engine

The Knowledge Engine uses embeddings to:

- Identify semantic neighbors  
- Detect clusters  
- Track conceptual drift  
- Measure theme density  
- Support semantic search  

Embeddings provide the semantic substrate.

## Knowledge and the Context Engine

The engine correlates concepts with:

- Emotional states  
- Cognitive states  
- Productivity patterns  
- Habit performance  
- Context shifts  

This enables context-aware conceptual insights.

## Performance

The engine is optimized for:

- Fast concept extraction  
- Incremental theme updates  
- Low-latency semantic search  
- Efficient cluster maintenance  
- Scalable conceptual storage  

It supports years of conceptual evolution.

## Summary

The Knowledge Engine gives Life.OS deep semantic understanding. It extracts concepts, detects themes, maps relationships, tracks conceptual evolution, and powers insights, reviews, the Life Graph, and long‑term identity modeling.
