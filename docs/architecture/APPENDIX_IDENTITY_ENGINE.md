# APPENDIX_IDENTITY_ENGINE.md  
**Identity Engine — Life.OS**  
*Part of the Life.OS Architecture Documentation Suite*

The Identity Engine models the user’s evolving sense of self: values, roles, long‑term themes, life chapters, internal narratives, and the deeper patterns that shape meaning and direction. It synthesizes conceptual, emotional, cognitive, behavioral, and contextual signals to understand *who the user is becoming* over time.

## Purpose

The engine provides a stable but flexible representation of identity. It helps Life.OS generate insights, recommendations, and reviews that align with the user’s values, aspirations, and long‑term themes—supporting growth without imposing labels or fixed categories.

---

## Identity Architecture

The engine uses a layered architecture:

- **Value layer** — core values inferred from long‑term patterns  
- **Role layer** — recurring roles across life domains  
- **Theme layer** — identity themes derived from conceptual clusters  
- **Narrative layer** — internal stories and meaning structures  
- **Chapter layer** — life chapters and transitions  
- **Evolution layer** — identity shifts and long‑term arcs  

Identity is treated as dynamic, contextual, and multi‑dimensional.

---

## Identity Data Model

The engine stores:

- **identity_profile_id**  
- **core_values** — inferred from long‑term behavior and reflection  
- **identity_themes** — conceptual clusters representing self‑narratives  
- **roles** — recurring life roles (creator, leader, learner, caregiver, etc.)  
- **narrative_patterns** — stories the user tells about themselves  
- **chapter_metadata** — life chapters and transitions  
- **identity_arcs** — long‑term developmental arcs  
- **supporting_evidence** — entries, insights, Life Moments, reviews  
- **evolution_metadata** — how identity shifts over time  

Identity is updated gradually and respectfully.

## Privacy and Safety

The engine ensures:

- Identity data remains fully local  
- No external profiling or categorization  
- No deterministic identity labels  
- Full user control over visibility and redaction  
- Identity insights are optional and user‑controlled  

Identity modeling is always supportive, never prescriptive.
