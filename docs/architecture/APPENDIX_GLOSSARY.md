# APPENDIX_GLOSSARY.md  
**Glossary of Terms — Life.OS**  
*Part of the Life.OS Architecture Documentation Suite*

This glossary defines all major terms, concepts, engines, modules, and intelligence features used throughout the Life.OS architecture. It serves as a reference for developers, designers, and contributors to ensure consistent language and shared understanding across the system.

---

# 1. Core Concepts

### Life.OS  
A local‑first personal operating system integrating journaling, notes, tasks, spreadsheets, intelligence, and visualizations into a unified platform.

### Local LLM  
A fully offline large language model running via WebLLM, powering insights, reviews, semantic search, and intelligence features.

### Life.Coach  
The intelligence orchestrator that generates insights, reviews, Life Moments, and cross‑module patterns.

### Life Graph  
A semantic graph representing all entities (entries, notes, tasks, concepts, habits, insights) and their relationships.

### Life Timeline  
A chronological record of all events across modules, including entries, notes, tasks, insights, and Life Moments.

---

# 2. Modules

### Journal  
The emotional and cognitive core of Life.OS, storing entries and generating emotional/cognitive insights.

### Notes  
The knowledge and idea‑capture module, supporting concepts, topics, and semantic linking.

### Tasks  
The productivity and execution module, supporting tasks, subtasks, projects, and productivity insights.

### Storage (Spreadsheets)  
The numeric and metric‑tracking module, supporting workbooks, sheets, formulas, and trend detection.

### Settings & Privacy  
Controls for user preferences, privacy modes, sync, notifications, and LLM configuration.

### Notification Engine  
Schedules and delivers reminders, insights, Life Moments, and context‑aware prompts.

---

# 3. Intelligence Engines

### Emotional Engine  
Analyzes mood, sentiment, emotional intensity, and emotional granularity.

### Cognitive Engine  
Detects cognitive distortions, thinking patterns, strengths, and identity themes.

### Habit Engine  
Detects habits, routines, streaks, and habit‑emotion/productivity correlations.

### Productivity Engine  
Analyzes task patterns, energy cycles, procrastination, and productivity rhythms.

### Knowledge Engine  
Extracts concepts, topics, clusters, and knowledge evolution patterns.

### Context Engine  
Detects temporal, emotional, cognitive, behavioral, and productivity context to power context‑aware suggestions.

### Visualization Engine  
Generates charts, dashboards, graphs, heatmaps, and timeline visualizations.

---

# 4. Intelligence Concepts

### Insight  
A structured piece of intelligence generated by Life.Coach, stored in `coach_insights`.

### Life Moment  
A meaningful emotional, cognitive, behavioral, productivity, or knowledge breakthrough detected by the system.

### Memory Consolidation  
A narrative summary of recent activity (daily/weekly/monthly/yearly) generated by Life.Coach.

### Semantic Search  
Search powered by embeddings, enabling concept‑based and topic‑based retrieval across modules.

### Embedding  
A vector representation of text used for semantic similarity, clustering, and graph edges.

---

# 5. Graph Concepts

### Node  
A semantic entity in the Life Graph (entry, note, task, concept, topic, habit, insight, moment).

### Edge  
A relationship between nodes (mentions, related_to, derived_from, influences, etc.).

### Cluster  
A group of related nodes detected by the Knowledge Engine or graph algorithms.

### Neighborhood  
The local subgraph around a node, used for exploration and insights.

---

# 6. Timeline Concepts

### Timeline Event  
A structured event representing something that happened in the user’s life (entry created, task completed, insight generated, moment detected).

### Timeline Arc  
A visualization of emotional, cognitive, or productivity patterns across time.

### Event Cluster  
A group of related events occurring close together in time.

---

# 7. Productivity Concepts

### Energy Cycle  
A pattern showing when the user tends to have high or low energy.

### Procrastination Index  
A score representing how often tasks are delayed or rescheduled.

### Completion Burst  
A period of unusually high task completion.

### Project Momentum  
A measure of how actively a project is progressing.

---

# 8. Emotional & Cognitive Concepts

### Emotional Granularity  
The level of detail in emotional expression (e.g., “sad” vs. “disappointed”).

### Emotional Weather  
Short‑term emotional patterns across days or weeks.

### Cognitive Distortion  
A biased or unhelpful thinking pattern (e.g., catastrophizing, black‑and‑white thinking).

### Identity Theme  
A recurring pattern in how the user describes themselves.

---

# 9. Knowledge Concepts

### Concept  
A semantic unit extracted from text (person, idea, topic, skill, value).

### Topic  
A cluster of related concepts forming a higher‑level theme.

### Knowledge Evolution  
How concepts and topics change over time.

### Concept Frequency  
How often a concept appears across entries and notes.

---

# 10. Habit Concepts

### Habit Event  
A recorded instance of a habit (e.g., journaling, exercise, meditation).

### Habit Loop  
Cue → routine → reward pattern detected by the Habit Engine.

### Habit Consistency  
A measure of how regularly a habit is performed.

### Habit–Emotion Correlation  
A relationship between habits and emotional patterns.

---

# 11. Data & Storage Concepts

### IndexedDB  
The local database storing all Life.OS data.

### Schema Version  
The version number of the IndexedDB schema, used for migrations.

### Sync Queue  
A list of operations waiting to be synced to the backend (optional).

### Operation‑Based Sync  
A sync model where changes are stored as operations rather than full documents.

---

# 12. PWA Concepts

### App Shell  
The persistent UI framework that loads instantly and wraps all modules.

### Service Worker  
A script that caches the App Shell and static assets for offline use.

### Manifest  
The configuration file defining PWA metadata (icons, theme, display mode).

---

# 13. LLM Concepts

### Insight Model  
The LLM used for emotional, cognitive, behavioral, and productivity analysis.

### Embedding Model  
A smaller LLM used for generating embeddings.

### Review Model  
A larger LLM used for generating narrative reviews.

### Model Quantization  
Reducing model size (e.g., Q4_K_M) for faster local inference.

---

# 14. Magic Features

### Cross‑Module Insight  
An insight combining data from multiple modules (e.g., emotion + productivity).

### Life Story  
A long‑term narrative generated from Life Moments and reviews.

### Identity Evolution  
A multi‑year pattern showing how the user’s self‑concept changes.

### Smart Notification  
A context‑aware notification triggered by emotional, cognitive, or behavioral patterns.

---

# 15. Summary

This glossary provides a shared vocabulary for the entire Life.OS ecosystem. By defining modules, engines, intelligence concepts, graph structures, timeline events, and LLM terminology, it ensures clarity and consistency across development, design, and documentation.
