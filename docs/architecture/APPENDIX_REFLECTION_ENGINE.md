# APPENDIX_REFLECTION_ENGINE.md  
**Reflection Engine — Life.OS**  
*Part of the Life.OS Architecture Documentation Suite*

The Reflection Engine generates prompts, questions, and guided self‑inquiry experiences that help the user deepen awareness, process experiences, integrate insights, and make meaning of their life. It draws from emotional, cognitive, conceptual, contextual, and identity signals to offer reflections that are timely, relevant, and supportive.

## Purpose

The engine strengthens the user’s ability to understand themselves. It supports journaling, review processes, Life Moments, and identity development by offering thoughtful, context‑aware reflections that help the user explore patterns, clarify intentions, and integrate growth.

---

## Reflection Architecture

The engine uses a layered architecture:

- **Signal layer** — collects emotional, cognitive, behavioral, and contextual signals  
- **Theme layer** — identifies relevant concepts, values, and identity themes  
- **Prompt layer** — generates reflection prompts and questions  
- **Context layer** — adapts reflections to the user’s current state  
- **Integration layer** — connects reflections to entries, reviews, and Life Moments  
- **Evolution layer** — updates reflection style based on user behavior  

This architecture ensures reflections feel personal, meaningful, and grounded.

---

## Reflection Data Model

The engine stores:

- **reflection_id**  
- **reflection_type** — emotional, cognitive, conceptual, identity, contextual, narrative  
- **reflection_text**  
- **supporting_signals** — insights, context states, concepts, Life Moments  
- **relevance_score**  
- **tone_metadata** — gentle, exploratory, grounding, clarifying  
- **delivery_channel** — journaling, review, notification  
- **user_feedback** — used, skipped, dismissed  

Reflections evolve based on user preferences and patterns.

---

## Reflection Types

The engine generates several categories of reflections:

- **Emotional reflections** — exploring feelings, needs, and emotional patterns  
- **Cognitive reflections** — examining clarity, beliefs, assumptions, and reasoning  
- **Conceptual reflections** — exploring emerging concepts or themes  
- **Identity reflections** — connecting experiences to values and identity themes  
- **Contextual reflections** — understanding situational influences  
- **Narrative reflections** — integrating experiences into a coherent story  
- **Goal reflections** — clarifying direction, progress, and alignment  
- **Routine reflections** — understanding daily and weekly rhythms  

Each type supports a different dimension of self‑understanding.

---

## Reflection Prompt Generation

Prompts are generated using:

- Emotional intensity and variability  
- Cognitive load and clarity  
- Habit and productivity patterns  
- Conceptual evolution  
- Identity themes  
- Life Moments  
- Review outcomes  
- Context shifts  
- Goal progress or obstacles  

Prompts are phrased in a supportive, non‑directive tone.

---

## Reflection Tone

The engine adapts tone based on:

- Emotional state  
- Cognitive load  
- Personality traits  
- Motivational drivers  
- Identity themes  
- Time of day  
- Recent context shifts  

Tone categories include:

- **Grounding** — stabilizing and calming  
- **Clarifying** — helping the user think clearly  
- **Exploratory** — encouraging curiosity  
- **Integrative** — connecting patterns and meaning  
- **Gentle** — supportive during emotional intensity  
- **Direct** — when clarity and focus are high  

Tone adaptation ensures reflections feel attuned and respectful.

## Privacy and Safety

The engine ensures:

- Reflections remain fully local  
- No external analysis or profiling  
- No prescriptive or therapeutic claims  
- Full user control over visibility and redaction  
- Reflection insights are optional and user‑controlled  

Reflections support awareness, not diagnosis or therapy.
